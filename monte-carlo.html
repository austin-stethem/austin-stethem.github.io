1. The Strategic Blindspot

Traditional pricing models rely on reactive intuition and flat margin structures. When a vendor issues a sudden price increase or freight costs spike, standard ERP systems often apply a blanket markup across the board. This creates massive risk: it instantly bleeds volume on highly sensitive anchor items (driving customers to competitors) while leaving margin on the table for elastic, low-risk parts.

2. System Architecture: The Closed-Loop Algorithm

To solve this, I architected an automated KVI Stratification engine integrated directly into a Python-based Monte Carlo stochastic model. This creates a closed-loop pricing system:

  • Phase 1: Geospatial KVI Identification & Meta-Modeling: The engine synthesizes 12-month rolling product velocity, localized demographic account data, and market sentiment to identify true Known Value Items (KVIs). Instead of manual line-by-line pricing, we establish a definitive "meta" price point for these core anchors, allowing single logic updates to scale across entire product families.
  • Phase 2: The Price Waterfall & Elasticity Bracketing: Before a price is finalized, the model executes a dynamic "Price Waterfall" that calculates hidden logistics overhead (freight spikes and surcharges). SKUs are then stratified into strict variance constraints. Group A (top 10% ultra-sensitive KVI anchors) is mathematically locked to a maximum 3% variance. Group B (core 40%) allows up to a 20% variance. Group C (highly elastic remaining 50%) permits up to 70% variance.
  • Phase 3: Stochastic Simulation & Recalibration: This bracketed data is fed into the Monte Carlo engine. By setting hard constraints on each waterfall tier, the model runs 50,000 randomized permutations to determine exactly how margins must be recalculated to hit total profitability targets following a market disruption.

3. Enterprise ROI & Actionable Defense

This engine transforms pricing from a static spreadsheet into a self-healing risk engine. It allows the enterprise to safely maximize profit capture on highly elastic items (Group C) while mathematically protecting the "P95 Worst-Case" margin floor on hyper-sensitive KVI anchor items (Group A). It utilizes a 6-month retrospective feedback loop, automatically auditing whether the market successfully absorbed the price change or if the elasticity brackets require immediate recalibration.
