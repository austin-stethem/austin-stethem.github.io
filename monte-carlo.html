Case Study: The Q3 "Flash-Crash" Margin Leak

The Issue: During Q3, our high-volume distribution network faced a dual-threat: an unforecasted 15% freight surcharge spike and a aggressive competitor price-war on tier-1 SKUs. Under our legacy reactive pricing structure, these costs were only realized at month-end, resulting in massive margin compression.

The Reactive Failure: In the old model, the pricing team waited for the P&L to show the damage. By the time the data was "clean," the revenue leakage was already baked into the monthly performance—a $70,000 loss that was impossible to recover.

The Predictive Fix: By running this Monte Carlo engine, we simulated the exact freight/competitor variance 3 weeks *before* the price list release. The model flagged the P95 Worst-Case scenario (the "Cliff" on the Cumulative Risk Curve). We initiated preemptive vendor negotiations for Net Purchase Discounts (NPD) and set proactive price floors, maintaining our margin integrity at 26.5% despite the market turmoil.
