1. The Problem: The Flaw of "Flat Margin" Pricing

In complex B2B distribution, margin is an illusion if it isn't weighted by volume. A standard ERP system might show a healthy 45% margin on a SKU at the P1 (Retail) level. However, if 80% of the actual transaction volume is moving through P3 (Jobber) accounts at a 22% margin, any flat pricing adjustment creates massive enterprise risk—bleeding volume on highly sensitive anchor items while miscalculating true profitability.

2. System Architecture: The P-Tier Break-Line Engine

To eliminate this blindspot, I architected a Dynamic Weighted Indexing engine that acts as a closed-loop pricing system:

  • Phase 1: Multi-Echelon Volume Stratification: The engine pulls 12-month rolling ERP data and scans the pricing waterfall (P1 List, P2 Cash, P3 Jobber, P4 Heavy Jobber). It identifies the exact "Break-Line"—the specific P-Tier where the majority of product volume is moving.
  • Phase 2: Weighted KVI Identification: Once the Volume Break-Line is established, the engine calculates the True Weighted Margin. SKUs with heavy Jobber (P3/P4) concentrations are automatically flagged as highly elastic Known Value Items (KVIs) and assigned strict variance limiters.
  • Phase 3: Stochastic Stress Testing: Before a price change is executed, the engine runs Monte Carlo risk permutations against the Weighted Margin, factoring in freight spikes and competitor deflation, ensuring our "P95 Worst-Case" floor is mathematically defended at the specific tier where the customer is actually buying.
