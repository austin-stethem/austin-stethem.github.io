1. Executive Project Summary & Business Context

Traditional enterprise pricing models often fail due to reliance on reactive, deterministic forecasting—calculating next year's margins based on last year's averages. In a high-volume B2B environment, this results in a "reactive cycle" where the pricing team spends 90% of their bandwidth manually correcting commodity SKU prices, leaving no capacity to manage the 10% of high-value fleet accounts that actually move the EBITDA needle.

To break this cycle, I engineered a Python-based Monte Carlo engine. By automating the routine risk management and margin defense for the commodity base, the model acts as a force multiplier, allowing the team to shift their human intelligence toward high-touch strategic engagement for our most profitable accounts.
