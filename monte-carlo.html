1. The Danger of Compounding Waterfalls

In B2B distribution, pricing is rarely a flat markup from cost. It is a compounding chain: P4 is built on P10, P3 is built on P4, and so on. Furthermore, front-end retail tiers (P2 and P1) are constrained by rigid psychological rounding rules and hardcoded gross margin divisors. This creates a massive mathematical vulnerability: a sudden cost spike at the P10 level violently ripples through the chain, often destroying the margin at the specific tier where the actual Jobber customer volume sits.

2. System Architecture: The Reconciliation Engine

  • Phase 1: Waterfall Construction: The engine pulls base P10 cost and cascades the pricing through P4 (Heavy Jobber), P3 (Jobber), P2 (Cash), and P1 (List). It enforces strict programmatic rounding logic (forcing P2 to .99 or .x9) and locks P1 to a strict 41% Gross Margin divisor to accurately mirror ERP reality.
  • Phase 2: Volume Break-Line Identification: It isolates the true B2B reality (where 95% of volume flows through P3/P4 Jobber accounts) and calculates projected annual profit based on that specific "Volume Break-Line."
  • Phase 3: Stochastic Re-Indexing: The engine stress-tests the compounding chain against landed-cost volatility. It calculates the exact revenue gap caused by the disruption and issues a prescriptive, mathematically perfect divisor adjustment on the Jobber tier to ensure the Annual Target Profit goal is successfully met.
