Executive Narrative

Enterprise pricing often relies on deterministic forecasting—calculating next year's margins based on last year's static averages. In a volatile supply chain, this is a failure point. This Python-based Monte Carlo engine stress-tests strategies against 50,000 randomized scenarios, providing probability distributions rather than static guesses.

Expected Margin = (Sale Price - (Base Cost * (1 + Competitor Erosion) + Freight Volatility)) / Sale Price

Case Study: The Q3 "Flash-Crash" Margin Leak

During Q3, our network faced unforecasted freight spikes and aggressive competitor price-matching. The Reactive Strategy failed to account for variance until margins were compressed. By the time the data was "clean," the revenue leakage was permanent. Our Predictive Fix simulated variance 3 weeks before the price list release, identifying the P95 Worst-Case and allowing us to preemptively hedge.
