1. The Strategic Blindspot

Traditional pricing models rely on reactive intuition and flat margin structures. When a vendor issues a sudden price increase or freight costs spike, standard ERP systems often apply a blanket markup across the board. This creates massive risk: it instantly bleeds volume on highly sensitive anchor items (driving customers to competitors) while leaving margin on the table for elastic, low-risk parts.

2. System Architecture: Mass-Scale Portfolio Stratification

To solve this, I architected an automated KVI Stratification engine integrated directly into a Python-based Monte Carlo stochastic model. Instead of evaluating SKUs in a vacuum, the system calculates the Blended Portfolio Margin by processing millions of SKUs simultaneously across three rigid logic tiers:

  • Group A (10% Volume - The Anchors): Ultra-sensitive Known Value Items (KVIs). These are mathematically locked to a maximum 3% variance to defend market perception.
  • Group B (40% Volume - The Core): Standard competitive parts. Allowed up to a 20% variance before the system hard-locks the price to prevent volume erosion.
  • Group C (50% Volume - The Elastic Tail): Blind or captive items. Permitted up to 70% variance. This group absorbs the shock of market spikes, subsidizing the margin lost by defending Group A.

3. Stochastic Simulation & Actionable Defense

When market volatility triggers the engine, it runs 50,000 randomized permutations against this 10/40/50 split. The output transforms raw pricing data into a self-healing risk engine, explicitly calculating the "P95 Worst-Case" blended margin floor to ensure total enterprise EBITDA targets are mathematically protected.
