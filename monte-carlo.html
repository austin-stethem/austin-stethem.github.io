Executive Narrative

Enterprise pricing models often rely on deterministic forecasting—calculating next year's corporate margins based on last year's static averages. However, in a volatile supply chain, averages mask catastrophic risk. To shift from descriptive reporting to predictive risk management, I engineered this Python-based Monte Carlo simulation. It utilizes vectorized operations (NumPy) to stress-test proposed pricing strategies against tens of thousands of randomized macroeconomic scenarios, providing executive leadership with probability distributions rather than static guesses.

$$\text{Expected Margin} = \frac{\text{Sale Price} - (\text{Base Cost} \cdot (1 + \text{Comp Erosion}) + \text{Freight Volatility})}{\text{Sale Price}}$$

Departmental Applicability

  • Finance & Pricing: Generates Risk-Adjusted Margin Floors. Instead of blanket 30% targets, finance uses P95 tail data to identify SKUs requiring higher margins to hedge against logistical spikes.
  • Sales Leadership: Validates price-match requests. If the simulation shows a high "Failure Probability" in the P95 tail, leadership has the empirical data to reject the price match or negotiate volume guarantees.
  • Executive Leadership: Quantifies "Revenue At Risk" to dictate capital allocation. It turns abstract supply chain chaos into a managed business risk.
