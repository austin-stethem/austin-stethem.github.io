1. Situation & Task: The Territorial Blindspot

The Enterprise Scenario (Step 1 of 5) A tier-1 vendor has announced an impending 15% cost increase across our core product lines. Simultaneously, aggressive competitors are encroaching on our high-value territories. Before we can mathematically model the financial risk or defend our margins in the ERP, we must first map the battlefield. We need to know exactly where we are exposed.

In high-velocity automotive and commercial distribution, networks typically rely on static, geometric mapping to define sales territories. This creates a fatal strategic blind spot: the assumption that geographic area equals revenue potential. A 20-mile radius in a congested urban core functions entirely differently than a 20-mile radius in an isolated rural corridor.

To move from descriptive analytics to prescriptive revenue capture, I architected a multi-layered Geospatial Intelligence Engine to strip away this "territorial blindness," utilizing Python API harvesters to programmatically identify over 1,200 competitor threats.

3. The Machine Learning Gravity Engine

Mapping raw points is only the first step. To translate geography into actual pricing strategy, I architected a POS Gravity Engine. By extracting 12 months of Point-of-Sale data (LTM Revenue) and Customer Ship-To addresses from the ERP, the Python engine mathematically measured the exact "as-the-crow-flies" distance between our fulfilling hubs and the customers generating the revenue.

This process established the Distance Decay Matrix, proving empirically that an urban branch might generate 90% of its revenue within a 5-mile radius, while a rural hub's revenue stretches across a 50-mile radius.

$$V = V_0 \cdot e^{-\beta d}$$ (The Gravity Model: Where V is projected sales volume, d is distance, and β is the Friction Coefficient determined by the POS data).

K-Means Topology Clustering

Finally, I fed the Distance Decay Matrix into a K-Means Unsupervised Machine Learning Model (via `scikit-learn`). The algorithm analyzed the mathematical decay curves of all 80 branches and automatically clustered them into 5 distinct Strategic Topology Tiers. This replaced subjective human guesswork with pure data, dictating exactly which branches could absorb aggressive price increases (Rural Monopolies) and which branches required defensive margin floors (Hyper-Dense Urban).
