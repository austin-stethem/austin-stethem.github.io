1. Executive Project Summary & Agile Business Context

The enterprise operated on a rigid, legacy pricing framework (Tiers A through E) tied to outdated geographic lines rather than real-time market elasticity. Executing a unified, flat-rate pricing matrix systematically leaves revenue uncaptured. Highly saturated urban corridors require aggressive, elastic pricing architectures to defend market share against direct brick-and-mortar competitors. Conversely, geographically isolated branches maintain highly captive, retention-driven markets capable of sustaining premium gross margin thresholds.

Faced with immediate revenue bleed in competitive corridors, I bypassed slow IT development cycles and architected a lightweight, high-impact Geospatial UI using custom Google Apps Script and Map integrations. This agile tool overlaid dynamic competitive data directly on top of legacy accounts, transforming a blind administrative process into a verifiable, spatially-aware intelligence engine that maximized EBITDA without requiring a massive software rollout.

2. The Geospatial Architecture & Data Integration

Blindly automating regional pricing overlooks critical ground-level operational nuances. Therefore, the architecture was intentionally designed as a custom spatial visualization layer to serve as the definitive "source of truth" for the pricing and sales teams.

THE CUSTOM SCRIPTING & UI ENGINE

I architected a custom Google Apps Script (JavaScript) backend to programmatically parse coordinate data, injection parameters, and custom map layers. This engine renders specific competitor iconography based on custom distance-vector queries, allowing stakeholders to visually toggle 10, 25, 40, and 50-mile competitor density radii with zero technical friction.

5-STATE COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE HARVEST

To ensure absolute data integrity, I spearheaded a multi-month, ground-up data enrichment campaign across five states. This initiative identified, verified, and manually mapped 100% of regional competitors—from major metropolitan hubs down to historically unmapped rural municipalities—creating a definitive, proprietary market footprint secured via role-based cloud access.

3. The Three Pillars of the Geospatial Engine

This visual matrix acts as the operational nervous system for three distinct corporate strategies, successfully bridging the gap between legacy operations and modern business intelligence.

PILLAR I: PRICING OPERATIONS & LEGACY INTEGRATION

Rather than relying on anecdotal field reports to assign new accounts, the pricing coordinator utilizes the spatial matrix to mathematically slot branches into the legacy A-E margin brackets.

Market Profile Spatial Reality (25-Mile Radius) Legacy Tier Translation Margin Strategy
Elastic Corridors High saturation (8+ competitors) Tiers A, B, E Aggressive market-share defense.
Captive Markets High isolation (0-2 competitors) Tiers C, D Premium margin retention.
Commodity Lines Universal visibility Uniform Tiers Strict parity; mathematically exempt from regional markups.
PILLAR II: SALES GOVERNANCE & KEY ACCOUNT DEFENSE

The map functions as a validation engine for sales governance. By integrating behavioral economics with spatial data, margin defense becomes a rigorous process. When a field salesman requests a localized discount on a key account—backing the request by accepting a direct reduction in their own commission—the pricing team utilizes the map to verify competitor density. If the matrix confirms heavy saturation, we authorize targeted, "loss-leader" price cuts on high-visibility commodities to protect the lifetime value of the overall account.

PILLAR III: CORPORATE EXPANSION & "WHITE SPACE"

By isolating internal storefronts against the total market saturation matrix, leadership utilizes the map to identify "White Space." The tool visually highlights rural communities that possess high fleet and demographic potential but lack immediate competitor presence, directly informing real estate and corporate expansion strategies.
