1. Situation & Task: The Illusion of Geometric Territories

In high-velocity automotive and commercial distribution, networks typically rely on static, geometric mapping to define sales territories. This creates a fatal strategic blind spot: the assumption that geographic area equates to revenue potential. A 20-mile radius in a congested urban core functions entirely differently than a 20-mile radius in an isolated rural corridor.

The Task: We needed to transition from descriptive analytics (identifying where competitors exist) to prescriptive revenue capture (quantifying exact market share risk). Mapping competitors without quantifying the actual Vehicles in Operation (VIO) caught in the crossfire leaves Pricing and Sales leadership reacting to margin erosion only after the volume has already been lost.

2. Action: Geospatial Architecture & System Mechanics

To eliminate "territorial blindness," I architected a multi-layered Geospatial Intelligence Engine. This tool cross-references physical asset locations against hyper-specific demographic data to calculate TAM (Total Addressable Market) and SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) in real-time.

A. The Haversine Distance Matrix

Rather than relying on flat geometric projections, the engine calculates the true spherical distance between our physical assets and active competitors. Every time the dashboard parameters are adjusted, the system recalculates the spatial matrix using the Haversine formula:

$$d = 2r \arcsin\left(\sqrt{\sin^2\left(\frac{\Delta\phi}{2}\right) + \cos\phi_1\cos\phi_2\sin^2\left(\frac{\Delta\lambda}{2}\right)}\right)$$

B. Isochrone Approximation via Topological Friction

Standard Euclidean radiuses fail in the real world. To simulate true "drive-time" Isochrones without incurring heavy third-party API costs for every render, the engine utilizes a Topological Friction Engine. Hubs and accounts are assigned a Topology Index. Urban centers enforce a severe friction penalty (multiplying the effective radius by 0.3 to reflect dense driving conditions), while Rural assets utilize a low-friction multiplier (1.5x) to acknowledge that commercial accounts will source parts from significantly further away.

C. Competitor Threat Weighting

Not all competitors impact our SOM equally. The algorithm assigns distinct mathematical penalties to active market players. A standard regional competitor carries a 1.0x baseline, while a known "Aggressive Discounter" carries a 2.5x threat penalty. If a discounter breaches an asset's radius, the Vulnerability Index spikes exponentially.

D. VIO Overlay & Market Capture Conversion

The ultimate goal is revenue mapping. The algorithm calculates the total population trapped within an asset's effective radius and extracts a 14.2% demographic modifier representing the "Aging Fleet" (e.g., 2011–2016 model year light/medium duty vehicles). By comparing this highly specific TAM target against our active Point of Sale (POS) transaction volume, the engine generates an instant Market Capture Percentage.
