1. Situation & Task: The Territorial Blindspot

The Enterprise Scenario (Step 1 of 5) A tier-1 vendor has announced an impending 15% cost increase across our core product lines. Simultaneously, an aggressive competitor is encroaching on our high-value territories. Before we can mathematically model the financial risk or defend our margins in the ERP, we must first map the battlefield. We need to know exactly where we are exposed and quantify the raw market potential surrounding our physical assets.

In high-velocity automotive and commercial distribution, networks typically rely on static, geometric mapping to define sales territories. This creates a fatal strategic blind spot: the assumption that geographic area equals revenue potential. A 20-mile radius in a congested urban core functions entirely differently than a 20-mile radius in an isolated rural corridor.

Furthermore, mapping competitor locations without quantifying the actual *human and vehicular volume* caught in the crossfire leaves Pricing and Sales leadership reacting to margin erosion only after the volume has already been lost. To move from descriptive analytics to prescriptive revenue capture, I architected a multi-layered Geospatial Intelligence Engine to strip away this "territorial blindness."

2. Architectural Methodology & System Mechanics

The engine operating underneath the dashboard UI utilizes four distinct algorithmic pillars to calculate live market vulnerability and generate automated action protocols:

A. Data Engineering: The VIO/POS Join

Before spatial rendering can occur, raw demographic data must be sanitized. I engineered SQL pipelines to extract internal Point of Sale (POS) volume from our ERP and join it directly against third-party Experian VIO (Vehicles in Operation) datasets using ACES/PIES industry standards. This ensures we are only measuring our exact Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM).

B. The Haversine Distance Matrix

Rather than relying on flat geometric projections, the engine calculates the true "as-the-crow-flies" spherical distance between our physical assets (Hubs and Accounts) and active competitors. Every time the dashboard parameters are adjusted, the system recalculates the matrix using the Haversine formula:

$$d = 2r \arcsin\left(\sqrt{\sin^2\left(\frac{\Delta\phi}{2}\right) + \cos\phi_1\cos\phi_2\sin^2\left(\frac{\Delta\lambda}{2}\right)}\right)$$

C. The Topological Friction Engine

Standard Euclidean radiuses fail in the real world. To simulate true "drive-time" Isochrones without incurring heavy third-party API costs for every render, the engine utilizes a Topological Friction Engine. Hubs and accounts are assigned a Topology Index. Urban centers enforce a severe friction penalty (multiplying the effective radius by 0.3 to reflect dense driving conditions), while Rural assets utilize a low-friction multiplier (1.5x) to acknowledge that commercial accounts will source parts from significantly further away.

D. Competitor Threat Weighting

Not all competitors impact our SOM equally. The algorithm assigns distinct mathematical penalties to active market players. A standard regional competitor carries a 1.0x baseline, while a known "Aggressive Discounter" carries a 2.5x threat penalty. If a discounter breaches an asset's radius, the Vulnerability Index spikes exponentially.
