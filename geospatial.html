1. The Problem: Territorial Blind Spots

In high-velocity automotive and commercial distribution, networks typically rely on static, geometric mapping to define sales territories (e.g., a "20-mile radius"). This creates a fatal strategic blind spot. A 20-mile radius in a congested urban core functions entirely differently than a 20-mile radius in an isolated rural corridor. To move from descriptive analytics to prescriptive revenue capture, I architected a multi-layered Geospatial Intelligence Engine to map actual competitor threats against our Hub & Spoke delivery network.

2. Machine Learning & K-Means Topology

Mapping raw points is only the first step. To translate geography into actual pricing strategy, I architected a POS Gravity Engine. By extracting 12 months of Point-of-Sale data (LTM Revenue) and Customer Ship-To addresses from the ERP, the Python engine mathematically measured the exact "as-the-crow-flies" distance between our fulfilling hubs and the customers generating the revenue.

I then fed this Distance Decay Matrix into a K-Means Unsupervised Machine Learning Model (via scikit-learn). The algorithm analyzed the mathematical decay curves of all branches and automatically clustered them into 5 distinct Strategic Topology Tiers. This replaced subjective human guesswork with pure data, dictating exactly which branches could absorb aggressive price increases (Rural Monopolies) and which branches required defensive margin floors (Hyper-Dense Urban).
