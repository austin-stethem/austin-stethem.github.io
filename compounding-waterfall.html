1. The Danger of Compounding Waterfalls & Sticky Pricing

In B2B distribution, pricing is rarely a flat markup from cost. It is a compounding chain: P4 is built on P10, P3 is built on P4, and so on. When a mid-year cost spike occurs at the P10 level (whether from vendor freight or internal operational overhead), companies often suffer from "Sticky Pricing"—failing to proactively recalculate the front-end market prices. Because the Jobber tiers (P3/P4) contain 95% of the transaction volume, even a slight delay in executing a divisor adjustment causes catastrophic margin bleed that compounding waterfalls cannot recover naturally.

2. System Architecture: The Reconciliation Engine

  • Phase 1: Waterfall Construction: The engine pulls base P10 cost and cascades the pricing through P4 (Heavy Jobber), P3 (Jobber), P2 (Cash), and P1 (List). It enforces strict programmatic rounding logic and locks P1 to a rigid 41% Gross Margin divisor to accurately mirror legacy ERP reality.
  • Phase 2: Volume Break-Line Identification: It isolates the true B2B reality (where 95% of volume flows through P3/P4 Jobber accounts) and establishes the target annual EBITDA based on that specific "Volume Break-Line."
  • Phase 3: Stochastic Re-Indexing & Market Tolerance: The engine simulates mid-year landed-cost volatility utilizing both external freight variance and internal overhead surcharges. It calculates the exact divisor adjustment required to bend the revenue curve back to the annual goal. Crucially, it deploys a Market Tolerance Filter: if the required adjustment exceeds acceptable market elasticity, the system strategically shifts from "Profit Recovery" to "Loss Minimization."
