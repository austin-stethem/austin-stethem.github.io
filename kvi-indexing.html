1. Executive Project Summary & Business Context

Historically, pricing adjustments at the enterprise level were reactive, driven largely by field-level complaints or sporadic customer feedback—a "loudest voice" heuristic that inherently lagged behind market volatility across our 80-branch network[cite: 1]. To eliminate this administrative drag, I architected and manage a Dynamic Weighted Indexing engine that shifts our pricing operations from passive spreadsheet management to proactive, forecast-driven margin defense[cite: 1]. By synthesizing usage volume, competitor pricing, and seasonal logistics cost-vectors, this system identifies Known Value Items (KVIs) and automates the pricing logic for entire product families[cite: 1].

2. The Logic of KVI Identification & Data Synthesis

Defining a Known Value Item (KVI) requires more than just analyzing sales volume; it requires a cross-functional consensus on what constitutes a "price-sensitive" anchor[cite: 1]. The KVI identification engine is a collaborative output between the Product Analytics team, Information Software leads, and the Pricing department[cite: 1]. We filter SKUs through a multi-variable funnel that includes 12-month rolling usage data, margin thresholds, and market sentiment-curated recommendations from branch managers and field sales agents[cite: 1].

3. Price Waterfall Mechanics & Competitive Meta-Modeling

Rather than performing manual, line-by-line price changes, I utilize a "Meta-Modeling" approach to pricing[cite: 1]. Once we establish a definitive "meta" price point for a core product range, the system automatically flows pricing adjustments to likeminded numbers within that product group[cite: 1]. This allows the pricing engine to scale exponentially; a single logic update ripples across the catalog, zeroing in on competitive gaps based on current market research[cite: 1].
